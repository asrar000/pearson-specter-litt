INTRODUCTION

Plaintiff PINNACLE TECH SOLUTIONS, INC. ("Pinnacle") brings this action against
Defendants QUANTUM DYNAMICS LLC ("Quantum") and its managing director HAROLD
WINSTED for breach of a Software Services Agreement dated September 1, 2023 (the
"Agreement"), and for fraudulent inducement in connection with that Agreement.

---

PARTIES

1. Plaintiff PINNACLE TECH SOLUTIONS, INC. is a corporation duly organized and
existing under the laws of Delaware, with its principal place of business at
1450 Broadway, Suite 2200, New York, NY 10018.

2. Defendant QUANTUM DYNAMICS LLC is a limited liability company organized under
the laws of the State of New York, with its principal place of business at
780 Third Avenue, Floor 12, New York, NY 10017.

3. Defendant HAROLD WINSTED is an individual and the managing director of Quantum.
Upon information and belief, Winsted resides in the State of New York.

FACTUAL ALLEGATIONS

4. On September 1, 2023, Pinnacle and Quantum entered into the Software Services
Agreement (the "Agreement"). A true and correct copy of the Agreement is attached
as Exhibit A.

5. Under the Agreement, Quantum agreed to deliver a custom enterprise data pipeline
system (the "System") capable of processing no fewer than 10,000 records per hour
with 99.9% uptime, by January 31, 2024. The total contract value was $2,850,000,
payable in three milestones.

6. Pinnacle paid the first milestone of $950,000 on October 1, 2023, and the
second milestone of $950,000 on December 1, 2023, for a total of $1,900,000
paid to date.

7. Despite receiving $1,900,000 in milestone payments, Quantum failed to deliver
the System by the January 31, 2024 deadline. As of the date of this Complaint,
Quantum has delivered no functional system whatsoever.

8. Prior to execution of the Agreement, Winsted represented to Pinnacle's CEO,
Jennifer Park, that Quantum had "successfully deployed identical systems for
Fortune 500 clients." Pinnacle has since determined that these representations
were false: Quantum had never completed a project of this scale.

9. Pinnacle made multiple written demands for delivery, including letters dated
February 5, 2024, and February 22, 2024. Quantum has failed to respond or cure
the breach.

10. As a direct result of Quantum's breach, Pinnacle has suffered damages
including: (a) $1,900,000 in milestone payments wrongfully retained by Quantum;
(b) approximately $450,000 in costs to engage a replacement vendor; and
(c) lost revenues estimated at no less than $3,200,000.

---

CLAIMS FOR RELIEF

FIRST CLAIM — BREACH OF CONTRACT (Against Quantum)

11. Plaintiff incorporates by reference the foregoing allegations.

12. The Agreement constitutes a valid and enforceable contract. Pinnacle performed
all conditions precedent. Quantum materially breached the Agreement by failing to
deliver the System by January 31, 2024.

13. As a result of Quantum's breach, Pinnacle has been damaged in an amount to be
proven at trial, but no less than $5,550,000.

SECOND CLAIM — FRAUDULENT MISREPRESENTATION (Against Quantum and Winsted)

14. Winsted's pre-contract representations that Quantum had deployed "identical
systems for Fortune 500 clients" were false and material. Winsted knew or should
have known they were false at the time they were made.

15. Pinnacle reasonably relied on these representations in entering the Agreement
and paying $1,900,000 in milestone payments.

16. Pinnacle is entitled to rescission and/or compensatory and punitive damages.

THIRD CLAIM — UNJUST ENRICHMENT (Against Quantum and Winsted)

17. Quantum has been unjustly enriched by retaining $1,900,000 in milestone
payments without delivering any contractual performance.
